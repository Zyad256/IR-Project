Information Retrieval: An Introduction

Information retrieval (IR) is the science of searching for information in documents, 
searching for documents themselves, and searching for metadata that describes data, 
and for databases of texts, images, or sounds.

Automated information retrieval systems are used to reduce what has been called 
"information overload". Many universities and public libraries use IR systems to 
provide access to books, journals, and other documents. Web search engines are the 
most visible IR applications.

Key concepts in information retrieval include:
- Indexing: Creating a structured representation of documents
- Query Processing: Parsing and understanding user search queries
- Ranking: Ordering results by relevance to the query
- Boolean Retrieval: Using AND, OR, NOT operators
- Vector Space Model: Representing documents and queries as vectors
- TF-IDF: Term frequency-inverse document frequency weighting
- Precision and Recall: Measuring search effectiveness

The field has evolved significantly with the advent of machine learning and 
natural language processing techniques, enabling more sophisticated understanding 
of both documents and user intent.
